Culture

The N Seoul Tower (Korean: N 서울타워), officially the YTN Seoul Tower and a.k.a. for the Namsan Tower, is a communication and observation tower located on the summit of Namsan Mountain (262m) in central Seoul, South Korea. The 236-metre-tall (774 ft) structure marks the second highest point in Seoul and is considered a local landmark.

Built in 1969, N Seoul Tower is South Korea's first general radio wave tower, providing TV and radio broadcasting in Seoul. Currently, the tower broadcasts signals for Korean media outlets, such as KBS, MBC, and SBS.
