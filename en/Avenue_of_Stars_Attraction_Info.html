Attraction

The Avenue of Stars is located in the Tsim Sha Tsui Promenade Gardens in the east of Tsim Sha Tsui, Kowloon. On May 23, 2003, New World Development Co., Ltd. announced a sponsorship of HK$40 million, sponsored by the Tourism Commission, the Hong Kong Tourism Board, the Leisure and Cultural Services Department and the Hong Kong Film Fund. Like award association support and comprehensive construction. The Avenue of Stars held an opening ceremony on April 27, 2004 and opened to the public the next day. It is managed by Hong Kong government departments. Starting from October 8, 2015, the Avenue of Stars will be closed until the end of 2018 for expansion projects, which will be carried out by the American landscape architecture firm James Corner Field Operations ("JCFO") and the British lighting design firm Speirs & Major (S+M) and local design team LAAB participated in the redesign. Later, due to engineering problems, the new Avenue of Stars was officially opened to the public on January 31, 2019 from the original end of 2018. The Avenue of Stars is a unique attraction that honors outstanding figures in the Hong Kong film industry. It was modeled after Hollywood Boulevard in the United States. The names and palm prints of outstanding film workers are inlaid on specially made commemorative plaques and are arranged on the Avenue of Stars according to age and order. Currently, the Avenue of Stars can accommodate 1,000 commemorative plaques.
