What is Singapore?

The Republic of Singapore (English: Republic of Singapore; Malay: Republik Singapura; Tamil: சிங்கப்பூர் குடியரசு, Romanized: Ciṅkappūr Kuṭiyaracu), also known as Singapore, Singapore, Singapore Jiapo, Lion City, Sin Chew Star Kingdom is an island country and city-state in Southeast Asia. It is located at the southernmost tip of the Indochina Peninsula and guards the southernmost outlet of the Strait of Malacca. It is separated from Indonesia by the Singapore Strait to the south and Malaysia from the Johor Strait to the north. It is separated by the Singapore-Johor Strait. Two bridges, the Causeway and the Second Channel, connect the two sides of Singapore and Malaysia. Singapore consists of 63 islands including Singapore Island, covering an area of ​​728 square kilometers. It is known as the Garden City due to its high degree of greening.

In 1819, Stamford Raffles, who worked for the British East India Company, signed a treaty with the Sultan of Johor and was allowed to establish a trading post and colony in Singapore. Through Raffles' efforts, it gradually developed into a prosperous entrepot. Due to its special geographical location, Singapore had been the most important strategic stronghold of the British Empire in Southeast Asia before World War II. From 1942 to 1945, Singapore was occupied by Japan for three and a half years. Later, it returned to British management and became independent from the Straits Settlements. It established an autonomous state in 1959 and joined Malaysia as an independent city-state in 1963, called the state of Singapore. (referred to as Xingzhou). However, on August 9, 1965, the Malaysian Parliament voted 126 in favor and 0 against, agreeing to expel Singapore from the Federation of Malaysia, allowing Singapore to establish an independent country and establish the Republic of Singapore.

Since the 1970s, Singapore's economy has developed rapidly and is known as one of the "Four Asian Tigers". Its per capita GDP and human development index rank among the best in the world. Currently, Singapore is an important oil refining, trading, and logistics center in the world, and it is also the largest international financial center in Asia. In 2014, Singapore's home ownership rate was 90.3%. In 2020, Singapore's population exceeded 5.6 million, making it one of the most densely populated countries in the world. Singapore is a multiracial, multicultural, and multilingual country, and its national construction is based on civic nationalism.
